Interactive Experience Research Project



A look at my experience interacting with the website for Downstream.

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to was the little code memorisation game. I was instantly intrigued by the little emoticon face and the fact that the little screen said “press [symbol of alien] to begin”. I felt like because of this interaction I ignored a lot of the other sections.

I also paid attention to the colour scheme of the website as it informed a lot of thematic information about what the website may be involved with.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • ♡ Sign up for their waitlist
  • ♡ Toggle sound
  • ♡ Adjust the memory games difficulty
  • ♡ Click and tear a playtest report out of a little receipt printer
  • ♡ View current playtest which is inactive
  • ♡ Click the little screen that has gameplay footage
  • ♡ Turn the console on and off
  • ♡ Click the information button
  • ♡ Click the discord link
  • ♡ Read about their game toggle open answers to questions

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Definitely the memory game on Mortons side. I found myself playing until I finished every level, and then I discovered the difficulty meter beside it and found that I played until I couldn't anymore!

I also spent a bit of time reading the information regarding the game on the information page. It seems like a really cute and fun game.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Most common action was repetitively clicking the buttons for the memory game in sequences. As you couldnt win the game, you could play again on a different difficulty, hence further clicking of the buttons.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal seems to be to get viewers interested enough to sign up for the games waitlist. Perhaps morton is also a character in the game, therefore you can create rapport with the game and its characters before being able to play.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

There is a large text box with scrolling text that reads “enter your email” with a sign up button underneath. I believe the website is wanting the target demographic of video game enjoyers to sign up for the developers early access, which is evidently no longer available on the website. It also has a subheading that says “access waiting list” Since this is the largest thing on the screen other than Morton’s memory game, I believe it to be the primary goal.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think you can access it once or twice, but since it is a very simple game, i believe users would get a bit bored after maybe 5-10 mins depending on the person. Once you have read and signed up to the waitlist you have basically completed all of the goals set by the website so it is not necessary to access the site again, unless there is an update to the game.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

I think it communicates when you can interact by the available playtest. As it currently says inactive, this would lead me to believe that there is the possibility for it to become active, so its possible there could be additions and updates to the website, however without this there is no reason to interact with the website again once you expend the attributes.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

It references the game 'Simon' which is a memory game where you remember colours in an order to win, and it gets progressively more complicated every round. This mini game on the website has the same exact principle, but with little symbols that perhaps reference the game they have designed and are intending to advertise on this website.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

I believe its wanting the user to be inquisitive, and playful. It suggests that whilst the game company and their projects are fun and entertaining, they also require some thought. It could also could reference the fact that their designs are to be enjoyed by all ages.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references communicate that perhaps this mini game is linked to something else that is large and interactive. The reference to 'Simon' the memory game suggests that this could be an interactive project that requires a bit of thought and perhaps strategy.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part was the fact that I had no idea what this websites initial purpose was as there was no real indicators or marketing of the game they were promoting. It took me far too long to realise that this was indeed promoting a sepereate company and creation, not just the mini game on the front page of this website.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

I think the most satisfying part of the interaction was winning the mini game at the highest level! I also feel like the design was incredibly cohesive and told a story that referenced the game companies core aesthetic choices and I feel like that it is a satisfying match in that regard.
