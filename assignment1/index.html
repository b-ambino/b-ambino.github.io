Interactive Experience Research Project


1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing i paid attention to was the little code memorisation game. I was instantly intrigued by the little emoticon face and the fact that the little screen said “press [symbol of alien] to begin”.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Play the little memory game under the morton side
  • Sign up for their waitlist
  • Toggle sound
  • Adjust the memory games difficulty
  • Click and tear a playtest report out of a little receipt printer
  • View current playtest which is inactive
  • Click the little screen that has gameplay footage
  • Turn the console on and off
  • Click the information button
  • Click the discord link
  • Read about their game toggle open answers to questions

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Definitely the memory game on Mortons side. I also spent a bit of time reading the information regarding the game on the information page. It seems like a really cute and fun game.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Most common action was repetitively clicking the buttons for the memory game in sequences. As you couldnt win the game, you could play again on a different difficulty, hence further clicking of the buttons.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal seems to be to get viewers interested enough to sign up for the games waitlist. Perhaps morton is also a character in the game, therefore you can create rapport with the game and its characters before being able to play.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

There is a large text box with scrolling text that reads “enter your email” with a sign up button underneath. It also has a subheading that says “access waiting list” Since this is the largest thing on the screen other than Morton’s memory game, I believe it to be the primary goal.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think you can access it once or twice, but since it is a very simple game, i believe users would get a bit bored after maybe 5-10 mins depending on the person. Once you have read and signed up to the waitlist you have basically completed all of the goals set by the website so it is not necessary to access the site again, unless there is an update to the game.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

I think it communicates when you can interact by the available playtest. As it currently says inactive, this would lead me to believe that there is the possibility for it to become active, so its possible there could be additions and updates to the website, however without this there is no reason to interact with the website again once you expend the attributes.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

It references the game that it is advertising, using footage from the game and imagery on both pages. It also references the simon memory game but with a different design, the symbols are more robotic looking. There is also an emoticon that responds to your clicking when playing the memory game.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

I believe its wanting the user to be inquisitive, and playful. You should engage based on touch and what is interesting to you. The game serves as a fun starting point for anyone who isnt aware of the game, and then you can click around and learn about the game and potentially sign up for the waitlist if you feel inclined.
